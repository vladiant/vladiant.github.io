## 🎯 **Your Career Focus**

You’re at a point where your **technical understanding**, growing **product intuition**, and visibility to both **business and engineering teams** can position you as a powerful cross‑functional communicator.  
This roadmap helps you become the person who can **translate business goals into engineering direction** — and vice‑versa.

***

# 🧱 **Phase 1 — Strengthen Core Communication Foundations (0–2 months)**

### **1. Cross‑Team Communication Foundations**

*   Learn to adjust messaging when talking to PMs, devs, design, and executives.
*   Practice explaining:
    *   a technical concept in business terms
    *   a business requirement in engineering terms
*   Build habits like using structured frameworks:
    *   **What–Why–How**
    *   **Problem → Impact → Proposal**
    *   **STAR** for presenting solutions

### **2. Meeting & Written Communication Mastery**

*   Improve your ability to lead alignment conversations.
*   Practice:
    *   Writing 1‑page summaries
    *   Turning feature ideas into clear problem statements
    *   Writing concise stakeholder updates

***

# 🧭 **Phase 2 — Product & Technical Fluency (2–4 months)**

### **3. Strengthen Product Management Understanding**

*   Learn:
    *   How PMs define goals, success metrics, OKRs
    *   How prioritization frameworks (RICE, MoSCoW) work
    *   How roadmap decisions are justified
*   Shadow PM planning sessions if possible.

### **4. Improve Technical Direction Awareness**

*   Deepen understanding of:
    *   Architecture basics
    *   Technical constraints that affect execution
    *   How developers estimate work
    *   How dependencies impact delivery
*   Begin participating more actively in refinement / grooming sessions.

***

# 🧲 **Phase 3 — Stakeholder Influence & Alignment (4–6 months)**

### **5. Executive Communication**

*   Practice speaking in terms executives care about:
    *   ROI
    *   risks
    *   timelines
    *   tradeoffs
    *   customer impact
*   Learn how to fit a message into a **60‑second executive briefing**.

### **6. Alignment Skills**

*   Lead a real cross‑team alignment effort:
    *   A feature delivery
    *   A process improvement
    *   A technical migration
*   Own communication between teams for that project.

***

# 🧩 **Phase 4 — Leadership Through Communication (6–12 months)**

### **7. Become the “Bridge” Between Technical & Business Worlds**

*   Proactively identify misalignments before they become blockers.
*   Facilitate:
    *   roadmap walkthroughs
    *   technical deep dives (with dev support)
    *   cross-team demos
*   Learn to express risks early with clarity and confidence.

### **8. Build a Reputation for Clear, Reliable Communication**

This phase is about *brand building*:

*   People should think:  
    **“If you are in the room, communication will be smooth.”**
*   Become known for:
    *   clarity
    *   structured thinking
    *   proactive alignment
    *   calm under pressure

***

# 🛠️ **Phase 5 — Advanced Skills for Future Growth (Beyond 12 months)**

### **9. Product Strategy Thinking**

*   Start participating in strategic conversations.
*   Learn how to:
    *   analyze customer problems
    *   think in terms of “value vs. effort”
    *   spot gaps in execution
    *   propose roadmap ideas

### **10. Technical Program Management (Optional Direction)**

If you enjoy coordination and technical alignment, this path fits:

*   Drive cross‑team initiatives
*   Create communication frameworks
*   Own risk mitigation
*   Improve engineering–PM workflows

***

# 📝 **Weekly Skill‑Building Rituals**

### **1. 30‑minute weekly review**

Ask yourself:

*   What went well in communication?
*   Where did alignment break down?
*   What could I clarify next week?

### **2. 1 cross‑functional coffee chat per week**

Build relationships with:

*   PMs
*   senior devs
*   QA
*   design
*   analysts
*   leadership assistants

Relationships = influence.

### **3. Monthly leadership simulation**

Choose one scenario:

*   Present a feature to executives
*   Write a roadmap update
*   Explain a technical risk to non‑technical audiences
*   Facilitate a mock planning meeting

***

# 📈 **Key Outcomes You’ll See**

By following this roadmap, you should notice:

### **After 3 months**

✔ You can articulate technical ideas more clearly  
✔ PMs trust your input  
✔ Developers see you as an organized communicator

### **After 6 months**

✔ You are leading alignment meetings  
✔ Executives trust you with updates  
✔ You prevent miscommunication before it happens

### **After 12 months**

✔ You’re seen as a cross‑team glue person  
✔ You influence prioritization and direction  
✔ You may be ready for PM, TPM, or team‑lead roles
