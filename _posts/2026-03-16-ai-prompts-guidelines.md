## Text prompts

### Before submitting, check:
* Context - does the neural network know who I am, who I'm writing for, and why?
* Role - have I told it how it should present itself?
* Format - have I specified the length, structure, and tone?
* Prohibitions - have I listed the don'ts?
* Examples - have I shown how it should look?
* Facts - am I going to double-check the numbers and names?
* Iterations - am I prepared to ask for 2-3 rewrites?

### After receiving, check:
* Style - does it sound like a living person or a soulless essay?
* Flavor - can it be shortened by 30% without losing the meaning?
* Facts - have I checked the links and statistics?
* Purpose - does the text solve the problem I set?

## Visual prompts

### Before submitting, check:
* Aspect ratio - did I specify --ar or choose a format?
* Style - did I choose a single style or am I hoping the neural network will figure it out on its own?
* Colors - did I limit the palette or allow a rainbow?
* Composition - did I say the entire object should be in the frame?
* Text - do I really want the neural network to write letters? (It's better not to)
* Negative prompts - did I prohibit crooked hands, extra fingers, and other mutations?

### After receiving, check:
* Anatomy - how many fingers? Are the eyes looking in the same direction?
* Composition - is anything important cut off?
* Text - if there are any text, are they legible?
* Style - is it a single style or a mixed bag?
* Details - do objects blend together?

## Why errors happen?
* They happen because we're too lazy to explain. 
* Too lazy to add context. 
* Too lazy to clarify the style. 
* Too lazy to check the facts. 
* Too lazy to ask for a redo.

## Three main takeaways
* Specificity is paramount. Don't just say "make it beautiful," but "make it like this, like this, in this style."
* The neural network is lying. Check the facts, even if it sounds convincing.
* The first version is not the last. Refine it, clarify, ask for revisions. The best result comes after 3-4 attempts.
